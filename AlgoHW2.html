How to Make Garlic Naan

Though restaurant naan is usually cooked in the intense heat of the tandoor oven, you can pull off this homemade version using a hot cast iron skillet. When cooked at the proper temperature, the naan will develop blistered bubbles with a lovely golden-black char. Reward yourself for a job well done by sopping it in copious amounts of curry.

Ingredients are as follows:

  • ½ cup warm water teaspoon white sugar
  • (.25 ounce) package active dry yeast
  • ¼ cup butter cloves garlic
  • minced cup plain yogurt
  • 2 cups bread flour
  • a teaspoon kosher salt
  • ¼ cup chopped cilantro (Optional)
>

Steps to Follow:

  1. Combine water, sugar, and yeast in a bowl. Let stand until yeast softens and forms a creamy foam, about 15 minutes.
  2. heat butter in a pan over medium heat until melted and sizzling. Quickly mix in garlic. Remove garlic butter from heat and set aside
  3. Add yogurt, bread flour, salt, and 1 tablespoon of the garlic butter to the yeast mixture.
  4. Stir with a wooden spoon until a shaggy dough forms. Knead by hand until dough pulls away from the sides of the bowl, adding more water or flour as needed.
  5. Place dough in a large bowl. Coat with a few more drizzles of garlic butter. Cover and let rise until doubled in volume, about 2 hours.
  6. Punch down dough and turn out onto the counter. Shape into a rough rectangle and cut into 6 pieces.
  7. Roll each piece into an oval about 1/8 inch thick. Sprinkle some cilantro on top and press lightly to adhere.
  8. Preheat a cast iron skillet until very, very hot, about 5 minutes. Cook each naan until large bubbles form, 1 to 2 minutes. Flip over, press gently, and cook until bubbles on the bottom are charred, 2 to 3 minutes more.
  9. Brush naan with more garlic butter before serving.

>

Enjoy your Garlic Naan with a delicious curry!!!
